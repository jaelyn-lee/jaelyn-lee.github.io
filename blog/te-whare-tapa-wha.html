Create a Wellbeing plan using Te Whare Rapa Wha model

Te Whare Tapa Whā is a model of the 4 dimensions of wellbeing developed by Tā Mason Durie in 1984 to provide a Māori perspective on health.

Wall Activities
Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing) Going to the gym after class 3 x a week
Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing) Calling my parents and Sister once every week
Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing) Having quality time with my friends who give me emotional and mental support
Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing) Going to church every Sunday and pray 3 times every
Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment) Going for a walk every day for 10 min
