Emotional Intelligence Exploration

EQ ad IQ

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence is a skill that you understand, process and express feelings, as well as recognise and be able to engage with the feelings of others. It is not to be learnt, it is to be trained by practising. It also includes having a healthy understanding of the emotions of others.

How is EQ different to IQ?

The intelligence quotient (IQ) is a number to represent a person’s overall mental ability. While IQ is critical for problem-solving, EQ is vital to understanding, perceiving others’ feelings and managing the relationship effectively. Scores on IQ tests have been linked to better academic performance and better jobs. But recent studies have demonstrated that high EQ has been linked to job success and better relationships in life and work.
In order to see the differences between EQ and IQ, see the table below.

IQ EQ
Knowledge of the world Identifying emotions
Visual processing Relating to others
Working memory Social communication
Measure of cognitive abilities Measure of emotional competency
Learn, understand and implement knowledge Perceive, recognise, control and express one’s own emotions
