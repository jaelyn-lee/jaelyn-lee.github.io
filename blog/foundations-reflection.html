Reflections

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

Get scared before I started, but don’t give up easily. And found coding very interesting

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

I think the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness are my root. It seems to help me not to fall easily and even if I have a hard and difficult time, I can get back up.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

Growth Mindset
I was thinking if it is possible for me to actually study these things and it applies to everyday life. But it gave me confidence just by thinking, "I can't do it yet, but I'll be able to do it soon," and gave me the ability to really do it.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Writing my thoughts in order was much more difficult than I thought.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I think because if you are not ready mentally, you cannot deal with the intense course after the foundation and also as mentioned in the handbook, employers want to hire people who can work with, rather than who are only good at coding.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

I really thought so until the beginning of the third week. I thought it was a process that I didn't have to do at that time, and I wanted to learn more technical things. However, when I got into the tech Sprint 3, I did core learning first and moved on to tech, and I thought that if I came to tech learning right away without core, this whole process would have been so overwhelming that it would have been difficult. That's why this core learning seems to give people who are new to coding, especially me, a firm mindset and prepare to jump into it industry.
